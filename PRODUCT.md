# Product

## Register

brand

## Platform

web

## Users

World Automotive Group speaks to three institutional audiences, weighted equally: investors and institutions assessing the group's scale and model, governments and regulators evaluating it as a serious national-scale operator, and carmakers, OEMs and commercial partners weighing whether to work with it. Their context is evaluation, not transaction. They arrive to judge whether this is a credible, durable operator and leave with an impression rather than a purchase. The site serves the people who decide whether to take the group seriously.

## Product Purpose

The corporate site for World Automotive Group, the automotive arm of Skelmore. Its job is to present the group as a serious institutional operator: five operating companies across the UAE and North America, run to one standard, built and productized rather than merely invested in. Success is an impression, not a click. A visitor should come away certain the group is real, sizable, and permanent.

## Positioning

Automotive, run like infrastructure. The single claim every screen reinforces: WAG builds and runs automotive operations at national scale, with the discipline and permanence of infrastructure rather than the volatility of retail.

## Conversion & proof

- Primary and secondary CTA: the goal is impression, not conversion, so the calls to action stay soft. Primary is Partner With Us, reaching the group office in Abu Dhabi; secondary is exploring the five operating companies and the group model.
- The line a visitor remembers after 10 seconds: automotive, run like infrastructure.
- Belief ladder: first, this is a real operator, not a concept; then, it runs proven operations across two regions; then, it holds those companies to one institutional standard; finally, it has the scale and heritage to be taken seriously by governments, investors and carmakers alike.
- Proof on hand: founded 1994 in Abu Dhabi; around USD 650M annual group revenue; around 4,000 people; 32 centres across two continents; five named operating companies with real businesses (FastTrack, AutoData, Axxion, PAG Direct, Vicimus); a heritage timeline from the 1990s to today; named group leadership. Every figure is verified and already on the site.

## Brand Personality

Institutional, striking, operator-grade. The voice is confident and understated, stating facts rather than selling them. The design is cinematic — a real-time neon field, near-black navy, a single orange accent — so the seriousness never reads as sterile. Visitors should feel trust, scale and permanence.

## Anti-references

Four things this must never resemble: generic corporate or consulting blandness, the stock-photo, muted-grey register; flashy consumer-startup energy, with gradient-hero hype, playfulness or emoji; anything templated or off-the-shelf that reads as a bought website; and a car dealership or retail showroom. It must also never reference any pending deal or partner that is not already public.

## Design Principles

Impression over conversion: every screen earns credibility, and there is no hard-sell funnel. Operator, not marketer: lead with real operations, real numbers and real companies, and let substance carry the page. Institutional, not sterile: striking, cinematic design signals seriousness without tipping into corporate blandness or startup hype. One group, many engines: five companies, visibly held to one standard. Verified only: never claim what isn't real, and trace every figure to its source.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Aims for WCAG 2.1 AA. The build already honors prefers-reduced-motion across the WebGL hero and the scroll reveals, ships a skip-to-content link, uses visible focus states and semantic structure, and keeps body text above 4.5:1 contrast on the dark ground. Motion is never required to reach content.
